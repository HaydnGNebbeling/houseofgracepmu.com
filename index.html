Before Your Appointment

How to Prepare

  • Please feel free to wear eyebrow makeup to the appointment — we can remove this once you arrive.
  • You do not need to wax or thread your eyebrows prior to the appointment. We can remove any excess hair during your session.
  • Please ensure you arrive on time for your appointment. If you are late, we cannot guarantee we will be able to see you.
  • You will be sent a medical and consent form before your appointment by email. Please ensure this is completed before you arrive.
  • Appointment time will be up to 3 hours — please plan your day accordingly.

Please Avoid the Following

2 Days Prior

Aspirin, Ibuprofen, alcohol, caffeine, energy drinks, and anti-inflammatory medication.

1 Week Prior

IPL treatments, electrolysis, and bleaching in the treatment area.

2 Weeks Prior

Botox, Retin A, AHA, chemical peels, and dermal fillers in the area to be treated.

Important Information

Contraindications

At House of Grace PMU, your safety and wellbeing are paramount. Permanent makeup is a highly specialised treatment, and in certain cases it may not be suitable. We kindly ask that you review the information below before booking your appointment.

When We Are Unable to Proceed

For your safety, we are unable to perform PMU treatments if you:

  • Are currently pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Are under the age of 18
  • Have any active skin conditions in the treatment area (including infections, rashes, or open wounds)
  • Are undergoing chemotherapy or radiotherapy
  • Have uncontrolled medical conditions, such as diabetes or epilepsy
  • Are taking prescribed blood-thinning medication without medical approval
  • Have a known allergy to pigments or topical anaesthetics
  • Have a tendency toward keloid or hypertrophic scarring
  • Have had recent surgery in the area to be treated
  • Have used Roaccutane (Isotretinoin) within the past 6–12 months

When Treatment May Require Guidance

In some instances, treatment may still be possible with medical consent or after an appropriate waiting period:

  • Autoimmune conditions
  • Controlled diabetes
  • Use of mild blood-thinning medications (e.g. aspirin)
  • History of cold sores (relevant for lip treatments)
  • Skin conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, or dermatitis
  • Recent Botox or dermal fillers (a minimum interval is required)
  • A tendency toward hyperpigmentation

A personalised approach. Every client is unique. A thorough consultation will always be carried out prior to treatment to ensure PMU is both safe and suitable for you. Where necessary, we may request medical clearance from your GP.


We kindly ask that all medical conditions, medications, and allergies are disclosed at the time of booking. This allows us to provide you with the highest standard of care and results.

Treatment Guide

Ombré Brows

Ombré Brows are a refined, advanced Permanent Makeup technique designed to create a soft, gradient effect through the brows. This treatment delivers a beautifully shaded finish, transitioning from a lighter, more diffused front to a deeper, more defined tail — mimicking the look of expertly applied makeup.

Perfect for those who desire a polished yet natural appearance, Ombré Brows can be tailored to suit your individual features, whether you prefer a subtle enhancement or a more defined, statement brow.

Combination Brows vs Ombré Brows — Know the Difference

The Technique

Using a precise digital device, pigment is gently implanted into the upper layers of the skin in a controlled, pixelated pattern. This creates a soft powder effect rather than individual hair strokes, resulting in a smooth, airy finish with long-lasting definition.

The treatment is fully bespoke. During your consultation, we carefully map your brows in harmony with your facial structure, bone anatomy, and natural brow growth. Pigment shades are custom-blended to complement your skin tone and hair colour.

Who It's Suitable For

  • All skin types, including oily or combination skin
  • Clients with sparse, uneven, or overplucked brows
  • Those who regularly fill in their brows with makeup
  • Anyone seeking a soft, powdery makeup look with minimal daily effort

Results & Longevity

Immediately after treatment, brows will appear more defined and slightly darker. As the skin heals, the colour will soften, revealing a beautifully diffused finish. Results typically last 1–3 years, with a top-up recommended to maintain colour and shape.

Comfort & Healing

Your comfort is prioritised throughout, with the use of high-quality topical anaesthetics to ensure a gentle experience. Healing is minimal, with slight flaking over 7–10 days and full results visible within a few weeks.

Treatment Guide

Combination Brows

Combination Brows offer the perfect fusion of artistry and precision, blending delicate hair strokes with soft shading to create a beautifully balanced, multidimensional brow. This advanced technique is ideal for those seeking both natural texture and refined definition.

By combining the realism of fine hair strokes at the front of the brow with a subtle powder effect through the body and tail, this treatment achieves a result that is effortlessly polished yet softly natural.

The Technique

This bespoke treatment utilises a combination of methods: ultra-fine strokes are carefully created to replicate natural brow hairs, while a digital shading technique is used to add depth, density, and structure. Each set of brows is individually designed, mapped in alignment with your facial features for perfect symmetry and proportion.

Who It's Suitable For

  • Clients with minimal to moderate natural brow hair
  • Those seeking a natural look with added definition and depth
  • Clients who enjoy a soft makeup finish
  • Normal to slightly oily skin types

Results & Longevity

Immediately following treatment, the brows will appear more defined and structured. As healing progresses, the colour softens and the strokes blend beautifully with the shaded areas. Results typically last 1–3 years, with maintenance appointments recommended.

Comfort & Healing

We prioritise your comfort at every stage, using premium topical anaesthetics to ensure a gentle experience. Healing is generally straightforward, with light flaking over 7–10 days and full results visible once the skin has completely settled.

Treatment Guide

Lip Blush

Every lip treatment begins with a detailed consultation, where we assess your natural lip tone, symmetry, and desired outcome. We will guide you in selecting the most suitable technique, ensuring your results feel balanced, refined, and in harmony with your overall features.

HD Lips vs Sheer Lips — Know the Difference

HD Lips

HD Lips is a highly specialised Permanent Makeup treatment focused on delivering a more defined, structured, and colour-rich result. Ideal for clients who wish to enhance both the shape and intensity of their lips while maintaining a sophisticated, polished appearance.

Using a precision digital device, pigment is layered into the lips in a controlled and methodical manner. This allows for greater colour saturation, refined border definition, and improved symmetry. Particular attention is given to the vermilion border and areas of uneven pigmentation.

Pigments are carefully selected and custom-blended to complement your natural lip tone and skin undertone. Where necessary, colour correction techniques can be incorporated to neutralise uneven tones.

Results typically last 2–3 years

  • Clients seeking a more defined and noticeable enhancement
  • Lips with uneven tone, pigmentation, or loss of colour
  • Those wanting to restore shape and symmetry
  • Clients who prefer a makeup-inspired finish without daily application

Sheer Lips

Sheer Lips is a delicate and understated lip enhancement designed to softly revive the natural lip tone. This treatment focuses on subtle colour enhancement, creating a fresh, healthy appearance without strong definition or saturation — similar to the effect of a hydrating lip balm or stain.

A lightweight layering method is used to deposit a sheer wash of pigment across the lips. The application is intentionally soft and diffused, with minimal focus on strong borders or sharp definition. Custom-blended pigments gently enhance your existing lip colour, adding warmth, brightness, or balance where needed.

Results generally last 1–2 years

  • Clients who prefer a very natural, low-maintenance look
  • Lips that appear pale, dull, or lacking vitality
  • First-time PMU clients seeking a subtle introduction
  • Those who rarely wear lipstick but want a healthy, even tone
Aftercare

Lip Healing Process

The healing journey is an essential part of your Permanent Makeup treatment. With lip procedures in particular, the skin is delicate and highly vascular, meaning changes in colour, texture, and appearance are completely normal as the lips recover. Understanding each stage will help you feel informed, confident, and reassured throughout the process.

Day 1 — Immediately After Treatment

Lips will appear more vibrant, defined, and slightly fuller due to both pigment saturation and natural swelling. The colour at this stage is intentionally stronger and brighter than the healed result. You may experience mild to moderate swelling, sensitivity, and a glossy appearance. This is a normal and temporary response.

Days 2–3 — Early Healing

Swelling will begin to subside, and the lips may feel dry, tight, or slightly textured. The colour will remain relatively strong during this stage. It is important to keep the lips hydrated using the recommended aftercare to support optimal healing.

Days 3–5 — Peeling Phase

The lips will begin to lightly flake or peel as the surface skin renews itself. Colour may appear uneven or patchy, and lips can feel dry or slightly chapped. It is essential not to pick or interfere with the skin — allowing the lips to heal naturally ensures even pigment retention.

Days 5–10 — Colour Fade

Once peeling has completed, the colour will appear to soften significantly — often lighter than expected. The lips may look more muted or slightly cooler. This is a completely normal part of the healing process as the pigment settles beneath the skin. This is temporary.

Weeks 2–6 — Colour Bloom

Over the following weeks, the colour will gradually return and stabilise, revealing the true healed result. The final tone will appear softer, more natural, and evenly distributed.

4–6 Weeks — Fully Healed

Full healing is typically achieved within 4–6 weeks. A perfecting session is recommended after healing to refine colour, enhance retention, and adjust any areas if needed. Every healing journey is unique, however when aftercare is followed correctly, the process is smooth and predictable.

Aftercare

Brow Healing Process

The healing process is a vital stage in achieving beautifully refined, long-lasting brows. As the skin recovers, it is completely normal for the colour, texture, and overall appearance to evolve. Understanding these stages will ensure you feel confident and informed throughout your healing journey.

Day 1 — Immediately After Treatment

Your brows will appear more defined, darker, and more structured than the final healed result. You may experience mild redness around the brow area, slight sensitivity, and a more finished or makeup-like appearance. This initial intensity will gradually subside.

Days 2–4 — Early Healing

The brows may begin to feel slightly dry or tight as the skin starts to heal. A light film may form over the brows and texture may feel slightly raised or dry. The colour will remain strong, and the shape will still appear bold. Keeping the area clean and following aftercare guidance is essential.

Days 4–7 — Flaking Phase

The skin will begin to lightly flake or shed as part of the natural renewal process. You may notice small flakes, temporary unevenness in colour, and a slightly patchy appearance. It is important not to pick, scratch, or disturb the area as this can affect pigment retention.

Days 7–14 — Colour Softening

Once flaking has completed, the brows may appear significantly lighter or softer than expected. The colour may look muted or less defined, and the overall result may appear softer and more natural. This is completely normal and temporary.

Weeks 2–6 — Colour Bloom

Over the following weeks, the pigment will gradually resurface and deepen slightly, revealing the true healed result. The brows will settle into a balanced, soft, and natural finish.

4–6 Weeks — Fully Healed

Full healing typically takes 4–6 weeks depending on your skin type and lifestyle. A perfecting session is recommended to reinforce colour, refine shape and symmetry, and ensure long-lasting results. Our techniques are designed to heal softly within the skin, ensuring your brows maintain a natural, elegant appearance over time.

Your 6-week perfecting session is recommended at this stage — £50.
